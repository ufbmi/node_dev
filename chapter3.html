Chapter 3

Insulin

Kara takes insulin at school

All the places insulin can be given

Insulin is given where there are areas of fat under the skin.



  • Back of the arms
  • Belly, but not too close to the belly button
  • Sides of the legs
  • Hips
  • Top part of the bottom

Different types of insulin

Short-acting insulin





  • Starts acting quickly between 15-30 minutes
    and usually wars off in 3-4 hours

  • Usually given right before eating
    or when glucose levels are high (3-4 times a day)

  • Short-acting insulins are:
    Humalog, NovoLog or Apidra
